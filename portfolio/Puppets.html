Jeffrey Lurie, AIA, LEED AP, Registered Architect

I started performing puppet shows when I was in second grade for my younger brother’s birthday parties, and then later turned my hobby into a business getting hired to perform at other kids parties, giving away a free performance at a library as my only startup marketing.  I wrote the scripts and then got my friends and family to record the voices onto a soundtrack that I then mixed with sound effects and music.  The shows were mostly based on common stories like Jack and the Beanstalk, the Three Little Pigs and Wind in the Willows. I also built the stage and made the scenery and the puppets.  After an 18 year break I returned to the stage for a one time only performance for my niece’s 5th birthday - writing and building a new show (in Orlando and then shipping it up to Philly) based on a children’s book “Sloth’s Birthday Party” that we had as children.   It was the only time I have performed live without a soundtrack - memorizing the 20 minute script and performing dressed in black rather than being hidden behind a stage as I had always done before.
