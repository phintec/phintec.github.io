Jeffrey Lurie, AIA, LEED AP, Registered Architect

In high school I was in charge of building the class float each year - and our class set a school record for taking 1st place all four years in a row.  I was in charge of the design and directed the weekend building parties when class members would show up to help paint and make tissue paper flowers.  The floats were built around a rental pick-up truck, and so the design had to be made in pieces so that we could put it all together on the truck in one afternoon.


We had almost no budget, so we used old refrigerator boxes for cardboard, paper mache and chicken wire, and discount mis-tint paint.


The first year involved a circus theme, with a large giraffe sticking out the top that we operated from inside so his neck would bob back and forth, and the lion and bear faces also nodded from side to side.


The second year we did the Wizard of Oz, and again we were inside moving the head back and forth and operating the jaw up and down - this year with dry ice effects.


The 3rd year was the 200th birthday of the Constitution, so we had Yankee Doodle in front of the Independence Hall - the tower of which could be lowered down inside everytime we passed under low electrical wires.


And our senior year was Alice In Wonderland, and again I was pulling strings from inside that manipulated the character heads to turn back and forth.
