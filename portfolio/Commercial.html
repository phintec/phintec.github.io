Concept study for Mills Avenue, Orlando.  This is a study for a combination of housing, retail and office, with angle parking bays above the retail.  This a theoretical study for what to do with small lots as an Urban Infill exercise.  The area is high traffic, but the existing buildings are at the end of their life, and the lots are only 100 feet deep, making a traditional two-bay 120 foot wide parking garage impossible.

Jeffrey Lurie, AIA, LEED AP, Registered Architect

Competition Entry for the “Sunshine State Sustainable Classroom.”  My proposal suggested stacking the standard “boxes” more in the line of modular construction rather than temporary and individual pods.  I showed a combination solar and vegetated roof and open breezeway style halls with natural cross-ventilation. This image was produced with a combination of cad, Sketch-Up and Photoshop.
